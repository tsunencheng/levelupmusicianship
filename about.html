About

Why this exists.

I'm Tsun-En (Nami) Cheng — I teach Years 7–9 music at Mount Lawley Senior High School in Western Australia. My teaching is Kodály-informed, and outside the classroom I also work as a film composer and serve on the committee at ABODA WA and AJEA.

Every game on this site started the same way: as a real classroom activity, trialled with real students, refined because something about a written worksheet wasn't landing the way the music itself did. Students can do rich musical work — singing in canon, deriving rhythm by ear, building part-work — and still walk out of a lesson feeling like nothing happened, because what they remember is "we did a worksheet."

I want students to leave a lesson feeling successful, engaged, and excited — not just like they completed a task.

That's the gap these games are built to close. They're not theory drills dressed up with a scoreboard. Each one is designed first as a live classroom activity with levels, minimal materials, and room for mistakes to be part of the learning — and where it makes sense, a digital version that travels beyond the one lesson it was built for.

This site exists so those games don't disappear after a single PD session or a single class. If you're a music teacher and something here is useful in your own classroom, that's exactly what it's for.
